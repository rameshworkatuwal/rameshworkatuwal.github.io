My Europe Journey — A Nepali Traveller's Diary
2025

Europe had always been a dream — the kind you pin on a mood board and wonder if it will ever really happen. In 2025, it happened. And nothing quite prepares you for the feeling of stepping off a plane in a continent you have only ever seen in films and travel vlogs.

The Beginning: Prague, Czech Republic

Prague was my first stop, and it set the bar impossibly high. The old town looks like it was pulled straight out of a fairy tale — medieval towers, winding stone lanes, and the Charles Bridge stretching over the Vltava River. I arrived in the evening, and the city was golden. I spent two days getting completely lost, and I would do it again without hesitation.

Amsterdam: Canals, Bicycles & Calm

Amsterdam surprised me with how peaceful it felt despite being a major city. I rented a bicycle on my first morning (as every visitor should) and cycled along the canal rings for hours. The city moves at a different rhythm — unhurried, open, and deeply human. The Rijksmuseum alone was worth the entire trip.

What I Learned on the Road

Travelling solo through Europe as someone from Nepal taught me a lot — about patience, about reading people across language barriers, and about how much the world has in common underneath all its differences. Every city had its own personality, and every stranger I sat next to on a train had a story worth hearing.

More chapters of this journey are coming — including Paris, Vienna, and a very cold morning in the Swiss Alps. Stay tuned.
